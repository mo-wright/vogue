This project analyzes historic issues of Vogue Magazine, sourced from their historical archives.

About the Team

Our team includes Tara, Molly, Trinity, and Miracle. We all wanted to look at how fashion trends changed within Vogue over the years. While working together, we each helped inspect different Vogue issues and label the fashion details in XML—from fabrics and colors to specific garments.

About the Project

For our project, we focused on the Vogue section “What She Wears.” We looked at issues from 1895 through 1995, marking up each description and illustration in XML to track what women were wearing in each decade.

We tagged materials, colors, and garment types, which helped us see patterns across the century and how they shifted. This let us understand how fashion—and the language Vogue used to describe it—changed over time.

Background

Vogue began in 1892, founded by Arthur Baldwin Turnure. It originally started as a newspaper documenting New York’s high-society fashion and lifestyle. From the beginning, Vogue reflected more than clothing—it reflected what was happening in the world.

In the early descriptions, we noticed more natural materials like silk, wool, and cotton compared to the synthetics we see today. Around the 1950s, materials shift, hat styles evolve, and feathers become less common. Beauty standards and cultural attitudes continue to change with each decade.

Main Goal

Our main goal is to show how Vogue documented the changing beauty standards over time. By examining a century of “What She Wears,” we were able to see how people responded to fashion, culture, and the expectations placed on women. Fashion always tells a story—and this project allowed us to uncover that story across 100 years.
